# CS 330 Portfolio Reflection

## How do I approach designing software?
I approach designing software by starting with a simple version of what I want to build and then improving it step by step. I try to understand the requirements first before adding any extra features. This helps me stay organized and avoid overcomplicating things early on.

## What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
This project helped me get better at planning how objects interact in a scene and thinking about structure before coding. I also learned how small design choices like placement, size, and color can change how a scene looks and feels.

## What design process did you follow for your project work?
I followed a build-and-test process. I started with a basic scene, tested it, and then made changes gradually. I focused on fixing one thing at a time instead of trying to do everything at once.

## How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
I can use the same approach in future projects by breaking tasks into smaller parts and building step by step. This makes debugging easier and helps keep projects organized.

## How do I approach developing programs?
I approach programming by writing simple working code first, then improving it over time. I test frequently to make sure everything still works after each change.

## What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?
I used incremental development, where I added features in small steps instead of building everything at once. This helped me avoid breaking the project and made it easier to find errors.

## How did iteration factor into your development?
Iteration was important because I kept testing and adjusting my scene as I worked. Each version improved slightly based on what I noticed needed fixing or changing.

## How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?
At first I focused on just getting things to work. Over time, I started focusing more on structure, readability, and making sure the code was organized and easier to manage.

## How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?
Computer science helps me build problem-solving skills and gives me the ability to create real applications. These skills are useful for future careers in software development or related fields.

## How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future educational pathway?
They help me understand how visual systems are built and how code can control graphics and movement. This gives me a better foundation for more advanced programming and graphics courses.

## How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future professional pathway?
These skills can be used in fields like game development, simulation, and software design. They also help me understand how interactive systems are built, which is useful in many tech careers.
